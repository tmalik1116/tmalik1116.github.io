Summer 2024 Work Term Report

Talha Malik - Student Project Coordinator

Chandos Construction

Chandos Construction is a Canadian General Contracting company, constantly tackling large new projects nationwide.

I was hired in order to help the Project Management team streamline the operations of a project in its final stages.

The founders named the company after Chandos Lake, - a body of water belonging to Ontario's Kawartha Highlands - a place that had a history in both of their upbringings.

Technologies Used

The company has large branches all over Canada, with regional head offices in Edmonton, Vancouver, Toronto, Chalk River, Red Deer, Calgary, and Ottawa.

I was employed by the Toronto division, stationed in the Aurora site office, and spent nearly the entire co-op term working on this project.

Although the position I held was not typically taken by Computer Science students, I introduced some of my unique skills and managed to tailor the job to my areas of expertise due to my strong initiative.

Job Description

The project I was assigned to for the duration of this work term was the total renovation of the Town of Aurora's Town Square. This included the library, a new cultural centre, a bridge connecting the two, and an ice rink in the courtyard.
The parts of this project that were relevant to me included coordinating outstanding tasks between trades, tracking deficiencies in the construction, and monitoring the physical status of the site. For these tasks I used Viewpoint's Team and Field View software, Microsoft Excel, and Openspace. These responsibilities did not have a high degree of relevance to my degree or the knowledge gained during my academic semesters. The majority of my academic development in this role has come from my own initiative and desire to enhance the work with my own skills and areas of expertise.
As of the 1-month mark, the majority of my daily work has involved programming of some kind. Many of the day-to-day tedious tasks our team needs to complete have now been automated by me using my existing knowledge of programming languages. In this job the language I used most frequently was Python, alongside useful libraries such as Pandas, NumPy, matplotlib, pynput, PyTorch, and GitHub for version control. These scripts were never prompted by my superiors but were universally impressive and welcomed.

Site Mock-up

Visible to the right is one of the useful scripts I designed, quite early on into the job.
Its original purpose was to read an Excel list of deficiencies in the new building, and input them into our construction management platform, Field View. It did this using a .csv version of the file, turning it into a Pandas DataFrame and automatically adding hundreds of entries into our database. With an average time of about 5 seconds per entry, this was monumentally faster than a member of our team needing to enter the data manually.
As decent as this was to start with, there were limitations and certain items required manual intervention. I continually iterated on this program, adding functionality such as:
- Reading directly from a PDF report without needing to convert to a CSV
- Extracting the location of each item from its description and inputting it accordingly
- Handling multiple files in succession without user intervention (passing it a list of filenames)

(I verified with my superiors that these clips were permissible to use and that no confidential information was present.)

Visible to the left is another useful tool I developed this summer.
There were numerous subtrades that were contracted by us, all of which had their own specific issues or incomplete work to be dealt with on site. My superiors wanted to continually remind them about the status of these items, so I realized that this would be another good opportunity to save time by automating the process.
This script carries out a complex series of steps, which would be more difficult for a person to carry out in such a repetitive manner. Although coding this did not pose a significant technical challenge, it still managed to save hours of our team's time due to the frequency at which these emails were to be sent out. It filters out each company's list of of ongoing tasks, downloads it as a PDF, and emails it to a given contact with a message specific to their company.

Overall Reflection

If I were to sum up my entire experience with Chandos up to this point, I would say that I have tried my best to maximise my career development in a non-ideal job. When I initially accepted this position, I felt like I had already wasted an entire Co-op term by not getting the sort of technical role I was looking for. Although for the first few weeks this remained true, I eventually became more motivated to try and develop myself as much as I could within the limits of the job. As soon as I started working on my numerous mini-projects, I enjoyed my work significantly more and was praised by my superiors for my technical skills. While I worked on these projects I learned how to use a multitude of new software libraries and gained even more experience testing code. I even started new personal projects outside of work as my set of skills and interests grew, with an example being how working with Pandas piqued my interest in ML, prompting me to learn PyTorch. Aside from technical skills, I did gain valuable communication and professional correspondence skills. Although not as marketable or personally interesting as being hands-on with areas of Computer Science, these are skills that will stay with me moving forward in my career and are helpful in all areas of life. I also gained connections with my superiors and left a very positive impression on them, resulting in a grade of Outstanding on my final performance evaluation and serving as another incentive for future employers when considering me. Although I do not plan to continue in construction after this year, I appreciate the value of having connections who would provide a very positive reference for future opportunities elsewhere. In the coming Fall 2024 Co-op term, I am continuing with Chandos and I will try my best to continue the momentum that I found midway through this term. I will continue to work on technical projects to improve my breadth of understanding at work and away from work, and will set myself up as well as I can for the Summer 2025 Co-op term.
